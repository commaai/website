1 Introduction

The official NeurIPS LaTeX style sets a 5.5 inch text width on letter paper, uses 10 pt Times Roman, and formats the title with a thick rule above and a thin rule below. Authors appear centered below the lower rule.

This HTML version approximates those choices: we fix the text width to match the LaTeX textwidth, use a Times-like font, and mimic the spacing around the title and abstract. The goal is to provide a simple template for project pages or web-friendly versions of NeurIPS papers.

You can freely replace the text here with your own paper content. Additional sections will continue to flow in the two-column layout.

Figures are centered within the column and constrained to the column width. The caption uses a slightly smaller font, similar to LaTeX’s default. You can float the figure by moving it earlier or later in the column’s HTML.

On very narrow screens the two columns may feel cramped; for a real site you might add a media query to stack them on mobile, but this minimal example intentionally stays close to the fixed-layout PDF feel.
